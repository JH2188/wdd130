Content

Home page

Hello! welcome to Hangar 15 a locally run and owned Utah Bike shop! We are excited to have you joining our crew of experienced and friendly staff. Remember our golden rule if you have a question don’t be afraid to ask. There is a lot of information to learn very quickly so sometimes asking can be your best resource. This website is designed to help you get on your feet your first few weeks on the job whether your role is a salesperson, technician, or even a manager. This site is designed to give you the resources to be successful in your role. There will be more information added to this page regularly so please check back often. {Here} is the basic layout of your shop. Just follow the link and select your store location. Some basic lingo to help you navigate the map: Layaways: Layaways are items ordered in for customers that we did not have right on hand the day the customer came in. Whether it was pulled in from another shop or ordered in from one of our vendors the layaway section is where you will find it. Work Order-To do’s: this section is where bikes that have come in for repair belong different areas in this section are for different purposes. To Do are work orders that have not been looked at or started. Waiting for parts: Workorders that are partially completed that need a part coming in from a vendor or another store. Warranty: this section is for Parts or bikes that have a manufacturer warranty submitted and are waiting for that part. Completed: This section as the name implies is for completed work orders waiting for the customer to come and pick up. {Here} are links a few to some helpful videos that cover basic customer interaction in the shop. If you are a brand-new employee please click {here} to print off your new hire paperwork once that is completed, give that paperwork and required documents to your department manager.

Sales

This page of the site is a quick resource for sales staff and should be checked often. There will be updated bulletins on price changes, inventory adjustments, sales tips, announcements on new and upcoming gear and equipment, and a way to chat between stores. This will also be your resource for quick reference spec sheets for all of last and current model year bikes, and components. There is a small section for notes for you to keep track of the answers to some of the more odd questions you might get from time to time. I.E calculating gear ratios, motor output for a given e-bike motor among other useful tips.

Images for the sales page

Service

This page of the site will be your hub for service reference, want to learn how to install and adjust that fancy new road drivetrain or fix that wheel that’s older that your grandma? This is the place for your to be. There are a few links to some external videos that walk you through getting started as a technician Remember we all had to learn the basics at some point, so ask questions and if you get stuck ask your head mechanic or service manager for help. There is also a section for you to keep notes for information you want to keep handy, but only use once or twice a year.
